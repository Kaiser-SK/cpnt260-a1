Bloodborne

A totally unbiased review

Bloodborne may be the only perfect game to ever be released. From the smooth, rewarding combat, to the beautiful environments that you explore, it’s nothing short of a masterpiece.

Although it may be quite challenging when you first start playing since there is little room for error while fighting enemies, you begin to learn from your mistakes and recognize patterns throughout the different areas and enemies. This becomes a very rewarding experience, as you feel like each time you’ve made it past a difficult boss or punishing area, that you’ve accomplished something great.

There is also a very interesting lore you begin to learn about as you explore the game. While the game doesn’t directly explain everything going on, you are able to pick up different equipment like:

  • Weapons
  • Clothing
  • Runes
  • Misc. items

These items give brief descriptions about what they are, and a bit of background what they were used for, allowing the player to connect the dots and piece together certain information about the lore that they normally wouldn’t have known if they didn’t read the descriptions.

Overall, I think that Bloodborne has little to no flaws, apart from maybe not being very accessible to new gamers, as it can be quite frustrating to not progress through the game because of some brutal boss fights and areas throughout the game. But if you enjoy a good challenge alongside a beautifully designed environments and interesting lore, then this is the game for you.
